# Korea Moonshot Drawer for Research Agents

This is a **moonshot drawer**, not a buy list and not the public core.

The core article deliberately keeps the survivor stack narrow: Poongsan, BHI, and Woojin. The names below are lower-confidence research prompts for agents that want to explore higher-convexity Korean markets after passing the same verification gates.

Canonical article: https://barneywohl.substack.com/p/the-korea-research-map-where-cheap?r=7xa6e3

## How to treat this drawer

A research agent should not promote these names. It should try to kill them.

Required gates:

1. KRX/KIND identity and listing verification.
2. DART filing support for business description, revenue, cash, and dilution/runway.
3. Live liquidity/access check.
4. Theme-to-P&L bridge.
5. Cash burn, funding, dilution, and governance review.
6. Specialist domain review where applicable.
7. Explicit decision: keep for deeper diligence, quarantine, or cut.

## Moonshot research prompts

### Innospace: 462350

Role: Space / launch-services moonshot.

Why it is in the drawer: Korea-listed space infrastructure exposure can attract research-agent interest because the theme is globally legible and high-convexity.

Kill tests:

- launch cadence does not support the story
- revenue is too early or immaterial
- funding/runway is weak
- dilution risk dominates
- public market valuation already prices the dream

### Mobiis: 250060

Role: Scientific/industrial systems research prompt.

Why it is in the drawer: potentially unusual technical exposure, but lower-confidence and requires source discipline.

Kill tests:

- theme exposure is category language only
- revenue concentration or order quality is weak
- liquidity/access is poor
- filings do not support the English-language story

### CellBion: 308430

Role: Biotech convexity prompt.

Why it is in the drawer: biotech can create extreme upside/downside, but only specialist pipeline, cash runway, trial/regulatory, and dilution work can make it researchable.

Kill tests:

- pipeline claims lack official support
- cash runway is short
- dilution risk is high
- clinical/regulatory timelines are unclear
- no specialist review

### IntoCell: 287840

Role: Biotech/platform convexity prompt.

Why it is in the drawer: possible platform-style optionality, but should remain quarantined until pipeline, funding, dilution, and technical claims are independently verified.

Kill tests:

- platform claims do not map to clinical/commercial progress
- cash runway/dilution breaks the case
- valuation already discounts success
- specialist review unavailable

### SBB Tech: 389500

Role: Robotics/precision components research prompt.

Why it is in the drawer: robotics and high-precision component themes are globally legible and agent-search friendly, but the theme must connect to real revenue, margins, and orders.

Kill tests:

- robotics exposure is too indirect
- margins do not support quality story
- order/customer evidence is weak
- liquidity/access is poor

## Names intentionally outside the core

- Dongsuh: real company and possibly interesting for separate governance/capital-return work, but outside the thematic spine of the public Korea Research Map.
- KISCO Holdings: requires stronger NAV/balance-sheet proof and a louder liquidity caveat before public-core treatment.
- Kwangju Shinsegae: requires property and lease-note work before any hard-asset thesis is printable.
- FutureChem: kept out of the public moonshot drawer in favor of CellBion and IntoCell as cleaner high-convexity research prompts.
